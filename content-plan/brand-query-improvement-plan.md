# VitalBloom Blog Brand Query Improvement Plan

Goal: help Google and readers understand that `VitalBloom Blog` is the official wellness publication at `https://www.vitalbloom.blog/`.

## Positioning

- Primary name: VitalBloom Blog
- Domain signal: VitalBloom.blog
- Tagline: Evidence-informed wellness guides for real life.
- Differentiation: free educational wellness publishing, not a product store, supplement brand, tea shop, skincare store, telemedicine service, or medical clinic.

## Site Signals Added

- Homepage H1 and header use `VitalBloom Blog`.
- Homepage includes an Official VitalBloom Blog section.
- `/vitalbloom-blog` explains the official site and what the publication is not.
- `/why-trust-vitalbloom` explains editorial standards, sources, disclaimers, corrections, and product independence.
- Footer links point to the identity and trust pages.
- Sitemap includes both new brand/trust pages.
- Organization and WebSite structured data use `VitalBloom Blog`, `VitalBloom.blog`, and wellness topic signals.

## Backlink Anchor Targets

Preferred branded anchors:

- VitalBloom Blog
- VitalBloom.blog
- VitalBloom wellness guides
- VitalBloom Blog wellness resources

Priority URLs for branded mentions:

- https://www.vitalbloom.blog/
- https://www.vitalbloom.blog/vitalbloom-blog
- https://www.vitalbloom.blog/why-trust-vitalbloom
- https://www.vitalbloom.blog/editorial-policy

## Social Entity Checklist

Create and maintain profiles only when they can stay active:

- Pinterest: VitalBloom Blog
- Facebook Page: VitalBloom Blog
- LinkedIn Page: VitalBloom Blog
- X/Twitter: VitalBloom Blog

Use the same bio everywhere:

> Evidence-informed wellness guides for nutrition, fitness, sleep, stress, mindfulness, and healthy living.

## Content Quality Direction

Competitors with similar names are mostly product or service sites. VitalBloom Blog should win trust by publishing:

- source-backed pillar guides
- printable-style checklists
- clear author and editorial notes
- correction pathways
- crisis/safety resources where needed
- category hubs with useful filtering
- no exaggerated product or medical claims
